# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data by pretending to be a trustworthy source. College students are often targeted through emails, messages, or fake websites that appear genuine.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives an email or message that looks like it is from a trusted organization, such as a bank, online service, or college administration. The message often creates urgency, such as asking the user to “verify their account” or “reset their password.” It usually contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the real one. When the user enters their login details or personal information, the attacker captures it.

Phishing can lead to serious consequences, including unauthorized access to accounts, financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data. Victims may also lose important academic or professional information. This case highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking links, verifying sources, and understanding common cyber threats to stay safe online.
