# Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a type of cyber crime in which an attacker sends an email to an individual in such a way that the individual shares their personal information, such as their password, bank details, or OTP, with the attacker. For example, consider a scenario in which a college-going girl named Shalini receives an email from what appears to be the university scholarship department. The email states that the payment for the scholarship of Shalini is “on hold.” It asks her to verify her student ID by clicking on the link provided in the email.

When Shalini clicks on the link, she is redirected to a page that looks exactly the same as the university’s official website. She enters her email ID, password, and later on, even the OTP, as she receives a “verification message.” Within a few minutes, the attacker gains access to Shalini’s email ID and changes the passwords of her online payment app and social media accounts using that email ID.

Students, working professionals, and elderly people are often victims of phishing attacks because they might fall for such emails. The repercussions of phishing can be financial, in which an individual loses their money, their accounts are hacked, their personal information is used for wrong purposes, they experience mental stress, and their personal information is misused.
